Detroit Crime Commission

My most recent professional experience was obtained last summer at the Detroit Crime Commission (DCC) as a Data Analyst Intern. The DCC was started by two former FBI agents, former FBI Detroit Division Chief Andy Arena, pictured right, and one of his right-hand-men Ron Reddy. The DCC works hand in hand with the Detroit Police Department and Detroit FBI as a civilian ally to the security, intelligence, and investigative processes. Some of my duties included direct assistence towards some security assessments for high-profile events in Detroit such as the Motorcity Pride 2019 parade. However, my primary responsibility was writing computer programs that converted their file lockers of criminal investigations and security assessments into a single, searchable, and compact data structure. At the end of the summer, I wrote a scraping algorithm that pulled relevant fields from thousands of reports in a little less than three minutes. I also wrote a GUI that assists DCC analysts in exploring the data cache that the scraping algorithm creates.
